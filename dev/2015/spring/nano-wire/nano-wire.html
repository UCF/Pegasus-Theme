Nano Wire

Cover

To protect and shield the coaxial cable, a layer of insulation is added.
X

Outer Electrode

A second layer of nanowhiskers is grown on copper foil that is coated with the electrolyte glue and wrapped around the separator. These outside whiskers hold a negative charge, and the combined sets of whiskers complete the electrical circuit.
X

Separator

Porous plastic is wrapped around the core wire and coated with an electrolyte gel that acts as glue and allows ions to trasfer between layers.
X

Nonowhisker Coating

Since copper oxide does not conduct electricity, conductive coatings of gold-palladium and manganese oxide are added to the nanowhiskers, allowing them to hold a positive charge.
X

Nanowhiskers

By heating the copper wire to 500 C, naonscopic copper oxide "whiskers" are grown on the exterior increasing the surface area, insulating the wire and preventing the loss of trasferring electricity.
X

Wire Core

At the core of the coaxial cable, a copper wire conducts electric current from one point to another.
