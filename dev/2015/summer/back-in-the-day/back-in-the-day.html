Back in the Day: Commencement Through the Years

In honor of our 250,000th graduate, we look back at 45 years of commencement ceremonies.
[social_buttons]

On May 9, UCF’s 250,000th graduate crossed the stage at the CFE Arena.

Daniel Berreth, ’15 , a U.S. Army veteran who served in Iraq and Kuwait, received his bachelor’s degree in marketing from the College of Business Administration and joined an alumni community one-quarter million strong. Since the first commencement ceremony in 1970, the locations, speakers and milestones have changed, but one thing remains constant — the satisfaction of earning a college degree.

Commencement Milestones

1970

First FTU commencement ceremony at Orlando Municipal Auditorium

1971

First master’s degrees awarded

1972–78

Commencement location: UCF Reflecting Pond (first ceremony on campus)

1974

UCF alma mater debuts

1975

Commencement location: Winter Park High School auditorium (winter ceremony)

1976

Commencement location: U.S. Naval Recruit Training Center Field House (winter ceremony)

1978

Trevor Colbourn becomes second UCF president

1980

First summer ceremony approved by President Colbourn and first doctorate awarded to Ali R. Hurson, ’80, in computer science

1981–88

Commencement location: Education Complex Gymnasium

1985

“NBC Nightly News” features UCF in class of ’85 segment

1987

UCF student-athletes achieve the best graduation record among Florida public universities

1992

John C. Hitt becomes fourth UCF president

1994–2006

Commencement location: The Venue (formerly UCF Arena)

1998

Commencement spans two days for the first time

2001

81-year-old Elmer Kundinger, ’98, earns his second UCF bachelor’s degree

2002

Tyler Fisher, ’02, becomes UCF’s first Rhodes scholar
