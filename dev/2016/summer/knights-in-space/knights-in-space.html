Knights In Space

Since our beginnings as one of America’s first space-grant universities in 1963, UCF researchers have focused their attention beyond our Central Florida skies to investigate cosmic questions on nearly every planet in the solar system, plus a few other celestial objects.

Mars

Curiosity

Researchers: Dan Britt

Objectives: To develop and build radiometric calibration targets that enable scientists to “see” Mars colors as they are on Earth.

Status: In progress (have been used on every Mars rover)

Mercury

Messenger

Researchers: Todd Bradley, principle investigator for Planteary Mission Data Analysis Program grant

Objectives: To learn more about the thin exosphere of Mercury, the smallest and swiftest planet in our solar system

Status: In progress

Astroid

Osirus-Rex

Researchers: Humberto Campins, co-investigator, and Yan Fernandex, contributing scientiest

Objectives: To send a spacecraft to the asteroid Bennu, one of the oldest in the solar system, and bring back samples to analyze.

Status: Scheduled to launch September 2016

Luna (The Moon)

Resource Prospector

Researchers: Philip Metzger, cooperating scientist

Objectives: To examine the soil on the moon’s poles in order to excavate potential resources including hydrogen, oxygen and water.

Status: Planning

Earth

Gold

Researchers: Richard Eastes, principal investigator

Objectives: To better understand solar wind’s impact on the Earth’s upper atmoshere.

Status: Scheduled to launch in 2017

Jupiter

Galileo

Researchers: Ramon Lugo ’79, NASA engineer

Objectives: To measure the atmosphere of Jupiter, the largest planet in the solar system, and observe the planet and its moons from orbit.

Status: Completed

Saturn

Cassini

Researchers: Joshua Colwell, co-investigator

Objectives: To observe, analyze and interpret data from Saturn’s rings using an ultraviolet imaging spectrograph.

Status: In progress

Uranus

Voyager 2

Researchers: Joshua Colwell, science team member

Objectives: To study the structure, origin and history of the rings of Uranus and Neptune.

Status: Completed

Neptune

Voyager 2

Researchers: Joshua Colwell, science team member

Objectives: To study the structure, origin and history of the rings of Uranus and Neptune.

Status: Completed

Kuiper Belt

New Horizons

Researchers: Stern, principal investigator, and Dan Britt, science team member

Objectives: To study the solar system’s outskirts and discover how ice dwarf planets have evolved over time.

Status: In progress

Upsilon Andromedae B

Spitzer Exoplanet Targets of Opportunity Program

Researchers: Joseph Harrington, principal investigator

Objectives: To measure the chemistry and temperature of the atmosphere of exoplanets, which are planets that orbit stars other than the sun.

Status: In Progress

Pluto

New Horizons

Researchers: Alan Stern, principal investigator

Objectives: To study the surface composition, atmosphere and temperature of the icy dwarf planet Pluto and its moon Charon.

Status: In progress
