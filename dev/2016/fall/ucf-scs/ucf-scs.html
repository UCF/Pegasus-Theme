The Knights lit up the scoreboard in their 2016 season opener against South Carolina State. The game marks the team's first win since 2014 and the first success for UCF’s new head coach Scott Frost, who joined the university’s athletic department late last year. With this win, Frost and the team’s offensive players rolled up the largest amount of yards gained in a game since 2014. And the defense secured the 23rd shutout by the Black and Gold in school history.

Coach Frost: You guys got us here, and I'm happy for you and I'm excited to watch the rest of the year. But we cannot forget what got us here, all right? What got us here was work, all right? Next week, we win or lose, the week after we win or lose, the week after we win or lose — let the chips fall where they may, but don't let any of that happen because we don't keep coming together as a team, and we don't go to work on Monday, Tuesday or Wednesday and we don't keep getting better.

Think about where are right now as a team, compared to where we have been. And then think how high can we go?

I'm really excited for you guys to see the way the rest of the season unfolds. Keep coming together and keep working.
